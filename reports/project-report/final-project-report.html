Depth of Field (Roger:5)

This implementation was based on the pbr book (Chp 6.2.3) and modifies the ray generation for the camera model to simulate a thin lens and aperture of non-zero radius. In comparison to the pinhole camera model, we do not always sample the ray from the origin. Instead we pick a random point on the aperture. In the simple case this is a uniformly random point on the unit disc, but this can also be made to be something else. We then connect this random ray origin point to the place where the unperturbed pinhole camera ray would intersect the focal plane.
This gives us two new parameters the tweak: the focal length/distance and the aperture size/lens radius. Objects at the focal length will be in focus. Larger aperture sizes will increase the blurring effect for objects further away from the focal distance. This can be observed in the test images below.
